# Analytics Dashboard

Track learner progress, measure course effectiveness, and gain insights.

## Accessing Analytics

1. Click **Analytics** in the main navigation
2. View the overview dashboard
3. Drill down into specific reports

## Dashboard Overview

### Key Metrics

**Active Learners**
Number of learners who accessed content in the last 30 days.

**Course Completions**
Total courses completed this month vs. previous month.

**Average Completion Rate**
Percentage of enrolled learners who finish courses.

**Average Quiz Score**
Mean score across all quizzes.

### Activity Graph
Visual timeline showing:
- Daily active learners
- Enrollments over time
- Completions over time

Toggle between 7 days, 30 days, 90 days, or custom range.

### Course Leaderboard
Top performing courses by:
- Completion rate
- Enrollment count
- Learner satisfaction

## Exporting Data

Download reports for offline analysis or integration with other tools.

1. Navigate to any report
2. Click **Export** (top right)
3. Choose format:
   - **CSV** — Raw data for spreadsheets
   - **PDF** — Formatted report for sharing
4. Click **Download**

## Scheduled Reports

Receive reports automatically via email.

1. Go to **Analytics → Scheduled Reports**
2. Click **+ New Report**
3. Configure:
   - Report type
   - Recipients (email addresses)
   - Frequency (daily, weekly, monthly)
   - Delivery time
4. Click **Save**

## Filtering Data

All reports support filtering:
- **Date Range** — Custom time period
- **Course** — Specific course or all courses
- **Learner Group** — Teams, departments, cohorts
- **Status** — Active, completed, dropped

## Understanding Metrics

### Completion Rate
`(Learners who completed / Total enrolled) × 100`

A healthy completion rate is 70%+. Below 50% may indicate:
- Course is too long
- Content isn't engaging
- Technical issues

### Engagement Score
Combines:
- Login frequency
- Time spent
- Quiz participation
- Resource downloads

Higher scores indicate active, engaged learners.

### Drop-off Rate
Where learners stop progressing. High drop-off at a specific lesson may indicate:
- Confusing content
- Technical problems
- Content too difficult
